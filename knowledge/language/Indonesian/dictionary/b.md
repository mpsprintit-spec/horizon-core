# Dictionary entries: B

## baik

- **Pronunciation:** /ba.ik/
- **Part of speech:** adjective; discourse response
- **Register:** neutral
- **Meaning:** good; well; okay as an acceptance response.
- **Usage:** As an adjective, it evaluates quality or condition. As a response, `Baik` can acknowledge an instruction politely.
- **Related words:** `kebaikan` (goodness), `memperbaiki` (to repair), `bagus` (good; nice), `buruk` (bad).
- **Examples:**
  - `Kondisi pasien baik.` — “The patient's condition is good.”
  - `Baik, saya akan menunggu.` — “Okay, I will wait.”
- **Cross-references:** pragmatics acknowledgment; conversation confirmation.

## bantu

- **Pronunciation:** /ban.tu/
- **Part of speech:** verb root
- **Register:** neutral
- **Meaning:** to help; to assist.
- **Usage:** Often appears as `membantu` in active formal clauses and `dibantu` in passive clauses. The root may appear in imperatives or compounds.
- **Related words:** `membantu` (to help), `bantuan` (help; aid), `pembantu` (assistant; helper), `tolong` (please help).
- **Examples:**
  - `Tolong bantu saya.` — “Please help me.”
  - `Relawan membantu warga.` — “Volunteers help residents.”
- **Cross-references:** grammar `meN-` and `di-`; conversation requests.

## bahasa

- **Pronunciation:** /ba.ha.sa/
- **Part of speech:** noun
- **Register:** neutral
- **Meaning:** language.
- **Usage:** Capitalize language names in English explanations, but Indonesian common usage writes `bahasa Indonesia` with lowercase `bahasa` unless sentence-initial.
- **Related words:** `berbahasa` (to use a language), `kebahasaan` (linguistic), `Indonesia`.
- **Examples:**
  - `Dia belajar bahasa Indonesia.` — “They are learning Indonesian.”
  - `Bahasa membantu manusia berbagi pengetahuan.` — “Language helps humans share knowledge.”
- **Cross-references:** examples metalinguistic examples.

## benar

- **Pronunciation:** /bə.nar/ or /be.nar/
- **Part of speech:** adjective; adverb
- **Register:** neutral
- **Meaning:** true; correct; right; really.
- **Usage:** Can evaluate factual correctness (`jawaban benar`) or intensify in some positions (`besar benar`, literary or regional in feel). `Benar` as confirmation may mean “that is correct.”
- **Related words:** `kebenaran` (truth), `membenarkan` (to correct; to justify), `salah` (wrong).
- **Examples:**
  - `Informasi itu benar.` — “That information is correct.”
  - `Benar, rapat dimulai pukul sembilan.` — “Correct, the meeting starts at nine.”
- **Cross-references:** conversation confirmation and correction; semantics antonymy.

## bisa

- **Pronunciation:** /bi.sa/
- **Part of speech:** modal verb; noun in another sense
- **Register:** neutral
- **Meaning:** can; be able to. As a noun, `bisa` can also mean venom.
- **Usage:** Context separates ability from venom. For ability, it precedes a verb: `bisa datang` (“can come”). For venom, it behaves as a noun: `bisa ular` (“snake venom”).
- **Related words:** `dapat` (can; may), `mampu` (capable), `kemampuan` (ability), `racun` (poison).
- **Examples:**
  - `Saya bisa membantu sekarang.` — “I can help now.”
  - `Bisa ular itu berbahaya.` — “The snake venom is dangerous.”
- **Cross-references:** semantics polysemy; grammar modal verbs.
