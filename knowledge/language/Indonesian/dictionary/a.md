# Dictionary entries: A

## ada

- **Pronunciation:** /a.da/
- **Part of speech:** existential verb; stative verb
- **Register:** neutral
- **Meaning:** to exist; to be present; to have in an existential sense.
- **Usage:** Often appears before a noun phrase to indicate presence. In possession-like clauses, the possessor usually comes first: `Saya ada waktu` means “I have time,” though `Saya punya waktu` is also common.
- **Related words:** `berada` (to be located), `keadaan` (condition), `tidak ada` (there is not; absent).
- **Examples:**
  - `Ada dokter di klinik.` — “There is a doctor at the clinic.”
  - `Saya ada pertanyaan.` — “I have a question.”
- **Cross-references:** grammar existential predicates; syntax subject-predicate clauses; semantics existence.

## air

- **Pronunciation:** /a.ir/
- **Part of speech:** noun
- **Register:** neutral
- **Meaning:** water; liquid water.
- **Usage:** Combines productively with classifiers and compounds: `air minum` (drinking water), `air hujan` (rainwater), `air panas` (hot water).
- **Related words:** `minum` (to drink), `cair` (liquid), `banjir` (flood).
- **Examples:**
  - `Tolong ambilkan air minum.` — “Please get drinking water.”
  - `Air sungai itu keruh.` — “The river water is cloudy.”
- **Cross-references:** semantics material nouns; examples basic requests.

## akan

- **Pronunciation:** /a.kan/
- **Part of speech:** aspect/modal marker
- **Register:** neutral to formal
- **Meaning:** marks future or intended action; “will” or “going to.”
- **Usage:** Indonesian does not inflect verbs for tense; `akan` makes future reference explicit when context alone is insufficient.
- **Related words:** `sudah` (already), `sedang` (currently), `mau` (want; going to, informal).
- **Examples:**
  - `Kami akan berangkat besok.` — “We will leave tomorrow.”
  - `Sistem akan mengirim pemberitahuan.` — “The system will send a notification.”
- **Cross-references:** grammar tense and aspect; pragmatics formal planning.

## aman

- **Pronunciation:** /a.man/
- **Part of speech:** adjective
- **Register:** neutral
- **Meaning:** safe; secure; free from danger.
- **Usage:** Used for physical safety, security, and acceptable risk. In Horizon knowledge, safety-sensitive interpretations should prefer explicit context.
- **Related words:** `keamanan` (security), `mengamankan` (to secure), `bahaya` (danger), `selamat` (safe; saved).
- **Examples:**
  - `Jalan ini aman untuk pejalan kaki.` — “This road is safe for pedestrians.”
  - `Pastikan data pengguna tetap aman.` — “Ensure user data remains secure.”
- **Cross-references:** semantics antonymy; pragmatics safety context.

## atau

- **Pronunciation:** /a.tau/
- **Part of speech:** conjunction
- **Register:** neutral
- **Meaning:** or.
- **Usage:** Connects alternatives. Inclusive or exclusive meaning depends on context unless clarified with wording such as `salah satu` (“one of”).
- **Related words:** `dan` (and), `maupun` (as well as), `atau tidak` (or not).
- **Examples:**
  - `Anda ingin teh atau kopi?` — “Do you want tea or coffee?”
  - `Pilih satu atau dua opsi.` — “Choose one or two options.”
- **Cross-references:** syntax coordination; semantics alternatives.
