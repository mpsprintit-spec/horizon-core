# Expressions Module

## Overview

The expressions module documents common Indonesian set expressions whose meanings are mostly understandable from their words but whose usage is conventional.

## Common expressions

| Expression | Meaning | Register | Usage |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `terima kasih` | thank you | neutral/formal | Standard expression of gratitude. |
| `sama-sama` | you are welcome | neutral | Response to thanks. |
| `maaf` | sorry; excuse me | neutral | Apology, softener, or attention-getter. |
| `permisi` | excuse me | neutral/formal | Entering, passing, interrupting politely. |
| `silakan` | please; go ahead | formal/neutral | Permission or invitation. |
| `tidak apa-apa` | it is okay | neutral | Reassurance or acceptance. |
| `hati-hati` | be careful | neutral | Warning or farewell wish. |
| `selamat pagi` | good morning | neutral/formal | Morning greeting. |
| `sampai jumpa` | see you | neutral | Closing or farewell. |
| `apa kabar` | how are you | neutral | Greeting question. |

## Usage notes

- `maaf` can soften disagreement or correction: `Maaf, saya kurang setuju.`
- `silakan` is more polite than a bare imperative in service contexts.
- `hati-hati` may be a genuine safety warning or a routine farewell depending on context.
